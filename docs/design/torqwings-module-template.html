What is Reynolds Number?

Before we design effective wings for small drones, we need the single most important dimensionless parameter governing their aerodynamic behaviour.

The formula

$$Re = \frac{\rho V L}{\mu}$$

Where V is the freestreamFreestreamThe undisturbed airflow ahead of the wing, unaffected by its presence. velocity, L is the chordChordThe straight-line distance from a wing's leading edge to its trailing edge. length, and μ is the fluid's dynamic viscosityDynamic viscosityA fluid's internal resistance to flow — its "thickness.".

The physical intuition

Reynolds Number is a ratio of forces — this is the insight that separates engineers who use Re from those who truly understand it:

$$Re = \frac{\text{Inertial Forces}}{\text{Viscous Forces}}$$

At low Re the two sides sit close in weight — viscosity keeps competing for control of the flow

Inertial (ρVL)

  • Momentum of the fluid
  • Flow "wants to keep going"
  • Sustains motion, resists change

Viscous (μ)

  • Internal friction — the fluid's "stickiness"
  • Damps disturbances
  • Keeps flow ordered and laminar
🌀

Engineering analogy

Pushing your hand through honey (high viscosity, low Re) versus through water at speed (low viscosity, high Re) — your drone wing sits somewhere on this spectrum.

What "low Reynolds number" means

Commercial aircraft cruise at Re of 10⁷–10⁸. Small drones operate in a very different band:

$$10{,}000 \leq Re \leq 200{,}000$$

Micro-insects & dust → small drones → transition zone → full-scale aircraft

  • ✅Not dominated by viscosity alone (unlike a microorganism at Re < 1)
  • ✅Not turbulent enough to behave like a large aircraft wing
  • ⚠️ Laminar Separation BubblesLSBA region where laminar flow detaches then reattaches turbulently, common at low Re. form readily and hurt performance
  • ⚠️Classic high-Re airfoil theory breaks down
⚡

Key takeaway

A wing that performs beautifully at Re = 10⁶ can perform catastrophically at Re = 50,000. Dedicated low-Re airfoil families — Eppler, Selig, Drela — exist precisely because of this.

Solve

$$Re = \frac{1.225 \times 10 \times 0.25}{1.789 \times 10^{-5}} \approx 171{,}000$$

Lands squarely in the low-Re design band from the previous section

This wing must use a dedicated low-Re airfoil, not a scaled-down commercial section.
